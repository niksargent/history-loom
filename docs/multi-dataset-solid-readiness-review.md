# Multi-Dataset Solid Readiness Review

## Purpose

Execute `md-task-05`: review Britain, USA, and France against the new `solid` threshold.

This is a higher-reasoning checkpoint.
The question is no longer whether the datasets are usable.
The question is whether any of them have stopped feeling provisional enough to count as `solid`.

## Threshold being applied

A dataset is `solid` when:
- source-backed event coverage is strong across the field
- watchlist periods no longer feel thin in live use
- the echo layer has been reviewed enough to separate strong, moderate, and exploratory confidence clearly
- lived voice reads like real human perspective rather than analyst prose
- the field can support further product extension without recurring structural repair

## Review outcome

### Britain

Judgment: `not yet solid`

Why:
- Britain is structurally strong and no longer needs broad enrichment
- the field works well as template dataset 1
- lived voice v2 is now materially better than the old single-voice layer

Blockers:
- Britain still carries the heaviest legacy draft burden
- several event additions remain model-authored draft rather than source-backed reviewed content
- several echo additions remain draft-scoped and still need stricter comparative review

Conclusion:
- Britain is close to solid in product usefulness
- Britain is not yet solid in validation confidence

### United States

Judgment: `not yet solid`

Why:
- USA is a strong usable-draft field with good anchors
- the event layer is source-backed enough for real use
- lived voice is now richer and more human

Blockers:
- watchlist periods `us-p01`, `us-p02`, and `us-p07` still deserve selective scrutiny
- the echo layer still contains exploratory or moderate-confidence items
- source-backed draft additions are accepted, but not yet elevated to a stronger reviewed state

Conclusion:
- USA is a strong usable draft
- USA is not yet solid because validation and comparative confidence are still uneven

### France

Judgment: `not yet solid`

Why:
- France now works well as a live third field
- the lens is coherent
- the seed is source-backed and the strongest anchor periods are compelling
- lived voice is now strong enough for public use

Blockers:
- France remains the newest field and has had the least cumulative validation time
- watchlist periods `fr-p03`, `fr-p06`, and `fr-p07` still need selective watching
- the more exploratory echoes remain explicitly provisional

Conclusion:
- France is strong enough to be live
- France is not yet solid because it still needs more validation depth than Britain or USA

## Cross-dataset judgment

No live dataset is yet `solid`.

That means:
- cross-dataset compare remains off
- the product should not yet imply equal confidence across all datasets

It does **not** mean the product must stop extending.

The product can still responsibly extend into:
- secondary exhibit views
- motion-led explanatory views
- additional non-comparative product surfaces

It should not extend into:
- cross-dataset compare
- high-claim automated similarity systems
- anything that implies canonically stable equivalence between fields

## Practical decision

The next step should be:
- continue selective validation toward `solid`
- but do not block lower-claim product expansion on that threshold

That means:
- `md-task-05` is complete
- `md-task-06` is now the correct next product step

## Summary

Current state:
- Britain: usable and structurally strong, but not yet solid
- USA: strong usable draft, but not yet solid
- France: live usable draft, but not yet solid

Project implication:
- the system is mature enough to keep evolving
- the data is not yet mature enough for cross-dataset comparison
