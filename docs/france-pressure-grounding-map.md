# France Pressure-Grounding Map

## Purpose

Define how the shared pressure model should be grounded across the first France seed before any runtime France dataset is created.

This is a higher-reasoning step because France is especially vulnerable to conceptual drift:
- regime change can be mistaken for the whole story
- famous events can crowd out structural pressures
- legitimacy, moral certainty, and state centralization can blur together if they are not separated carefully

## Pressure posture for France

The shared pressure model is still usable for France.
No extra France-specific pressure is required yet.

But not every shared pressure should carry equal weight in the first seed.

## High-salience France pressures

These should be treated as first-order pressures in the France seed:
- `institutionalLegitimacy`
- `socialCohesion`
- `moralCertainty`
- `militarization`
- `publicHope`
- `inequality`

These are central because France is repeatedly defined by:
- refounding and collapse of political order
- conflict over who belongs to the nation
- class and ideological polarization
- war and state crisis

## Secondary but important pressures

These should remain in the model, but not drive every era:
- `informationAcceleration`
- `technologicalDisruption`
- `individualAutonomy`
- `economicPrecarity`

These become more salient in later modern France, especially:
- mass press and political scandal
- consumer modernity
- postwar media society
- platform-era legitimacy strain

## Lower-salience starting pressure

- `ecologicalStrain`

This should stay in the shared model, but it is not a first-pass organizing pressure for France seed v1.
It may appear later through specific modern events, but it should not distort the seed around a weak signal.

## Era-by-era grounding plan

### Era 1 — 1789-1799
`Revolution and radical refounding`

Primary pressures:
- institutional legitimacy
- moral certainty
- public hope
- social cohesion

Grounding logic:
- Bastille and the Declaration ground collapse-and-refounding legitimacy
- the Terror grounds moral certainty under emergency

### Era 2 — 1799-1815
`Consulate, empire, and continental war`

Primary pressures:
- institutional legitimacy
- militarization
- public hope

Grounding logic:
- 18 Brumaire grounds executive consolidation
- Napoleonic Code grounds durable order through state structure
- Waterloo grounds the collapse of legitimacy-through-glory

### Era 3 — 1815-1848
`Restoration, monarchy, and recurrent rupture`

Primary pressures:
- institutional legitimacy
- social cohesion
- public hope

Grounding logic:
- the Restoration grounds unstable settlement
- the July Revolution grounds recurrent rupture and limited refounding

### Era 4 — 1848-1871
`Republic, empire, and defeat`

Primary pressures:
- institutional legitimacy
- moral certainty
- militarization
- social cohesion

Grounding logic:
- 1848 grounds democratic opening
- Bonapartist consolidation grounds executive-authoritarian legitimacy
- 1870 defeat grounds collapse through war and national humiliation

### Era 5 — 1871-1914
`Third Republic, secular nation-building, and industrial society`

Primary pressures:
- institutional legitimacy
- inequality
- moral certainty
- social cohesion

Grounding logic:
- Third Republic settlement grounds durable republican legitimacy
- Dreyfus grounds fracture inside the republic
- 1905 grounds secular civic identity and moral conflict over the republic

### Era 6 — 1914-1940
`War, instability, and republican fracture`

Primary pressures:
- militarization
- social cohesion
- institutional legitimacy
- inequality

Grounding logic:
- 1914 grounds mass national mobilization
- the Popular Front grounds reform under strain
- 1940 grounds catastrophic legitimacy collapse

### Era 7 — 1940-1958
`Occupation, liberation, Fourth Republic, and empire crisis`

Primary pressures:
- institutional legitimacy
- militarization
- public hope
- social cohesion

Grounding logic:
- Vichy and resistance ground contested legitimacy
- liberation grounds public hope and refounding
- Fourth Republic grounds unstable parliamentary legitimacy
- 1958 crisis grounds collapse under empire and executive weakness

### Era 8 — 1958-1981
`Fifth Republic, decolonization, and technocratic order`

Primary pressures:
- institutional legitimacy
- public hope
- social cohesion
- moral certainty

Grounding logic:
- Fifth Republic constitution grounds strong-executive stability
- Algerian independence grounds decolonizing recalibration
- May 1968 grounds revolt against technocratic order and inherited authority

### Era 9 — 1981-2025
`European integration, identity strain, and fragmented legitimacy`

Primary pressures:
- institutional legitimacy
- informationAcceleration
- social cohesion
- inequality
- moral certainty

Grounding logic:
- Mitterrand grounds reform and left-governance expectations
- Maastricht and 2005 referendum ground Europe-sovereignty strain
- 2015 attacks ground security, identity, and emergency legitimacy

## Pressure warnings

### `institutionalLegitimacy`

This is the most important France pressure.
It must not become a vague synonym for “important political event.”

Use it only where events genuinely alter:
- regime stability
- constitutional order
- public acceptance of the state

### `moralCertainty`

Use carefully.
It should reflect:
- ideological absolutes
- revolutionary or secular-republican conviction
- polarized certainty about the nation’s moral path

It should not just mean “people cared strongly.”

### `publicHope`

Use only where there is a real opening or sense of collective possibility:
- 1789
- 1848
- liberation
- some early Fifth Republic moments

Do not inflate it in periods that are primarily reactive or coercive.

### `informationAcceleration`

This is mainly later-modern in France seed v1.
Do not overproject it backward merely because political language is intense.

## Seeding rule

Before runtime seed files exist, every proposed France event should answer:
- which primary pressure does this ground?
- which secondary pressure does it illuminate, if any?
- is it doing unique structural work or duplicating another candidate?

If an event does not improve pressure grounding, it should be questioned.
