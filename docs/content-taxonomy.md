# Content Taxonomy — The History Loom

This document defines the conceptual categories used throughout the experience.

## 1. Purpose

The taxonomy gives the product a consistent way to describe eras beyond raw events. It ensures periods can be compared using the same language.

## 2. Primary taxonomy groups

### A. Values
What ideals, norms, or priorities were culturally dominant?

Examples:
- duty
- honour
- piety
- obedience
- liberty
- self-expression
- sacrifice
- ambition
- security
- stability
- exploration
- efficiency

### B. Social mood
What did the era feel like?

Examples:
- confidence
- dread
- exhaustion
- fervour
- humiliation
- possibility
- resentment
- optimism
- fatalism
- restlessness

### C. Structural condition
What broad conditions shaped life?

Examples:
- high legitimacy
- collapsing trust
- rising inequality
- fragmented elites
- strong cohesion
- technological acceleration
- ecological strain
- militarized order

### D. Emergence
What became newly possible or visible?

Examples:
- new art forms
- new institutions
- literacy expansion
- mobility
- bureaucracy
- mass participation
- scientific method
- consumer abundance

### E. Loss
What quietly diminished or broke?

Examples:
- local autonomy
- shared belief
- craft continuity
- social rootedness
- privacy
- stability
- mutual obligation
- intergenerational continuity

### F. Human condition
How did life change for ordinary people?

Examples:
- more opportunity but more precarity
- greater mobility but weaker rootedness
- lower violence but higher alienation
- more freedom but less certainty

## 3. Pressure taxonomy

The starter pressure list is:
- inequality
- elite competition
- institutional legitimacy
- social cohesion
- technological disruption
- information acceleration
- ecological strain
- militarization
- moral certainty
- public hope
- individual autonomy
- economic precarity

## 4. Scale taxonomy

Every period and event should support one or more scales:
- personal
- local
- national/state
- global/civilizational

## 5. Event type taxonomy

Suggested event types:
- political
- military
- social
- economic
- religious
- cultural
- technological
- ecological
- demographic
- intellectual

## 6. Echo taxonomy

Echoes should be taggable by the kind of similarity being claimed.

Examples:
- confidence before overreach
- institutional decay under prosperity
- fragmentation followed by demand for order
- spiritual unrest amid abundance
- liberation followed by instability
- post-trauma renewal
- acceleration without adaptation

## 7. Gain/loss framing

Each period should support a paired framing:

### What this era made possible
- freedoms
- forms of beauty
- cooperation
- innovation
- dignity
- abundance
- agency

### What this era quietly destroyed
- continuity
- rootedness
- limits
- solidarity
- skill traditions
- faith structures
- predictability
