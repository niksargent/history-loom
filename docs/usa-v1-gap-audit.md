# USA V1 Gap Audit

## Purpose

Define the bounded work needed to move the United States dataset from `seed` to `usable`.

## Current baseline

- 8 periods
- 22 anchor events
- 7 draft echoes
- 8 snapshots
- minimum multi-dataset app loading already works

## Coverage read

Before the first usable-enrichment pass, the USA seed was strongest in:
- `us-p04`
- `us-p08`

It was thinnest in:
- `us-p02` on dispossession and state-backed expansion
- `us-p07` on the neoliberal labour-order break
- `us-p08` on late ecological/public-health shock

## First usable-enrichment pass

This pass adds:
- `us-e19` Indian Removal Act
- `us-e20` PATCO strike and Reagan's firing of air traffic controllers
- `us-e21` COVID-19 pandemic in the United States
- `us-e22` Dust Bowl

These additions improve:
- territorial expansion and exclusion in `us-p02`
- labour restructuring and neoliberal turn in `us-p07`
- ecological strain and system shock in `us-p08`
- ecological strain and material fragility in `us-p05`

## What usable means for USA

The USA dataset should be treated as `usable` when:
- every period has at least two meaningful anchor events
- the thinner structural eras have a third anchor where it materially improves interpretation
- no major pressure is present in the model but absent from the event layer
- every period has at least one echo and one snapshot
- draft vs validated content remains explicit

## Remaining gaps after this pass

The main remaining weaknesses are no longer raw event count. They are:
- deciding whether the current snapshot layer is now accessible enough or needs a richer lived-voice layer

## Recommended next steps

1. Decide whether the next accessibility gain should be a richer lived-voice layer rather than more snapshot rewriting.
2. If yes, define how that layer should fit the current schema and detail flow.
3. Only then decide whether more structural expansion is justified.
