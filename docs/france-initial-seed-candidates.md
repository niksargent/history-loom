# France Initial Seed Candidates

## Purpose

Provide the first anchor shortlist for France seed v1.

These are planning candidates, not runtime data yet.

## Era 1 — 1789-1799
`Revolution and radical refounding`

Candidate anchors:
- Storming of the Bastille (1789)
- Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen (1789)
- Reign of Terror / Year II radicalization (1793-1794)

Why these matter:
- legitimacy collapse and refounding
- rights language and civic universalism
- revolution moving from promise into coercive emergency

## Era 2 — 1799-1815
`Consulate, empire, and continental war`

Candidate anchors:
- Coup of 18 Brumaire (1799)
- Napoleonic Code (1804)
- Waterloo and imperial collapse (1815)

Why these matter:
- executive consolidation
- legal order and state centralization
- military expansion and legitimacy through glory

## Era 3 — 1815-1848
`Restoration, monarchy, and recurrent rupture`

Candidate anchors:
- Bourbon Restoration settlement (1815)
- July Revolution (1830)

Why these matter:
- unstable legitimacy after empire
- monarchy repeatedly forced to renegotiate the political order

## Era 4 — 1848-1871
`Republic, empire, and defeat`

Candidate anchors:
- February Revolution and Second Republic (1848)
- Coup of Louis-Napoléon Bonaparte / Second Empire (1851-1852)
- Franco-Prussian War and collapse of the Second Empire (1870)

Why these matter:
- democratic opening and closure
- executive-authoritarian return
- defeat as regime-ending shock

## Era 5 — 1871-1914
`Third Republic, secular nation-building, and industrial society`

Candidate anchors:
- Constitutional Laws of the Third Republic (1875)
- Dreyfus Affair (1894-1906)
- Law on the Separation of Churches and State (1905)

Why these matter:
- durable republican settlement
- legitimacy under fracture
- secularism and civic identity as state structure

## Era 6 — 1914-1940
`War, instability, and republican fracture`

Candidate anchors:
- French entry into World War I (1914)
- Popular Front government (1936)
- Fall of France (1940)

Why these matter:
- mass war and national sacrifice
- social reform under pressure
- catastrophic regime fracture

## Era 7 — 1940-1958
`Occupation, liberation, Fourth Republic, and empire crisis`

Candidate anchors:
- Vichy regime and Free French resistance (1940-1944)
- Liberation of Paris / provisional refounding (1944)
- Constitution of the Fourth Republic (1946)
- May 1958 crisis and collapse of the Fourth Republic (1958)

Why these matter:
- collaboration versus resistance
- legitimacy rebuilt after defeat
- parliamentary instability and imperial crisis

## Era 8 — 1958-1981
`Fifth Republic, decolonization, and technocratic order`

Candidate anchors:
- Constitution of the Fifth Republic (1958)
- Algerian independence (1962)
- May 1968 uprising and general strike (1968)

Why these matter:
- strong executive refounding
- decolonization and state recalibration
- social and cultural challenge to established order

## Era 9 — 1981-2025
`European integration, identity strain, and fragmented legitimacy`

Candidate anchors:
- Election of François Mitterrand (1981)
- Maastricht Treaty referendum (1992)
- 2005 referendum on the European Constitutional Treaty
- 2015 attacks and state-of-emergency politics

Why these matter:
- left reform and later market constraint
- Europe as legitimacy and sovereignty question
- identity conflict, security politics, and fragmented public order

## First-pass seeding note

Do not include every candidate in the first runtime seed.

The right first seed should likely choose:
- 2 anchors for some eras
- 3 anchors for the most structurally dense eras

The shortlist above is deliberately broader than the first runtime seed so later selection can remain disciplined.
