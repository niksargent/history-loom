# France Snapshot Strategy

## Purpose

Define how the first France dataset should gain human texture before any runtime France seed is created.

France needs this layer from the start.
Without it, the dataset will read too much like a sequence of regime changes and not enough like a lived historical field.

## Recommendation

Start with:
- one primary snapshot per period
- one lived-voice response per period

Each period should therefore carry:
- a plain-language structural snapshot
- a more direct human response that translates the era into everyday stakes

## Tone

The France snapshot layer should be:
- plain-language
- vivid but disciplined
- accessible to younger and non-specialist audiences
- historically grounded without sounding theatrical

The lived-voice layer should feel like:
- a person speaking from inside the time
- not cosplay
- not faux dialect
- not a lecture in disguise

## What the lived layer should do

For each period, the lived layer should help the user feel:
- what ordinary stability or instability feels like
- what the state is asking of people
- what feels dangerous, hopeful, unfair, or exhausting
- whether the period feels expansive, coercive, brittle, or fractured

It should translate structural tensions into human terms.

## Suggested perspective mix

Across the 9 France eras, vary the implied standpoint so the dataset does not collapse into one social voice.

Suggested mix:
- revolutionary urban household, artisan, or market participant
- Napoleonic conscript, clerk, or provincial citizen
- restoration-era worker, smallholder, or town resident
- 1848 or Second Empire republican, labouring resident, or Paris household
- Third Republic teacher, clerk, Jewish family member, or industrial worker
- war and interwar veteran household, parent, or wage earner
- occupied household, resistant, refugee, or rationed civilian
- early Fifth Republic civil servant, factory worker, student, or Algerian-war observer
- late-modern suburban commuter, precarious worker, student, immigrant-background citizen, or digitally saturated voter

## Era emphasis

### Era 1 — 1789-1799
Make the user feel:
- collapse of old order
- opening, fear, and moral certainty together
- politics entering ordinary life at full force

### Era 2 — 1799-1815
Make the user feel:
- order restored at a cost
- the reach of the state
- ambition, discipline, and war pressing into daily life

### Era 3 — 1815-1848
Make the user feel:
- unsettled settlement
- partial calm without deep legitimacy
- ordinary life shadowed by the sense that rupture may return

### Era 4 — 1848-1871
Make the user feel:
- hope rising quickly and being contained or broken
- strong executive order as both relief and limit
- defeat and humiliation arriving at the civic level

### Era 5 — 1871-1914
Make the user feel:
- republican stability becoming normal
- school, state, and secular identity shaping daily life
- fracture still living underneath civic order

### Era 6 — 1914-1940
Make the user feel:
- sacrifice, loss, and exhaustion
- a republic under cumulative strain
- reform attempts inside a brittle national mood

### Era 7 — 1940-1958
Make the user feel:
- occupation, scarcity, fear, and moral ambiguity
- the promise of liberation
- unstable reconstruction and empire strain

### Era 8 — 1958-1981
Make the user feel:
- stronger state order
- modernization and technocratic confidence
- generational and cultural revolt against inherited authority

### Era 9 — 1981-2025
Make the user feel:
- fragmented legitimacy
- identity strain and media saturation
- everyday uncertainty inside a formally stable republic

## Guardrails

Do not let the France lived layer become:
- a Paris-only story
- a sequence of elite constitutional summaries
- a caricature of Frenchness
- a substitute for factual structure

The point is not to perform identity.
The point is to make structure legible through ordinary life.

## Immediate output for France seed v1

Create:
- 9 snapshots
- 9 lived-voice entries
- one plain-language structural synopsis per period
- optional internal notes on implied viewpoint and confidence

## Status

This strategy completes the France planning layer.
