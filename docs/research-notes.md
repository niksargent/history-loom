# Research Notes — Britain Seed Dataset

This dataset is a **curated seed pack**, not a claim that British history has one universally accepted periodization.

## Why the 80-year lens?
- The span from **1066 to 2025** is **960 years**, which divides neatly into **12 equal 80-year periods**.
- That makes it a useful first design lens for a recurrence-oriented interface.
- The period labels are interpretive summaries placed on top of those fixed 80-year blocks.

## Historical anchors used

The seed summaries and event choices were informed by a small set of well-established constitutional, political, social, and economic anchors, including:
- the Norman Conquest and Norman reordering of England
- Magna Carta and the longer development of parliamentary constraint
- the late medieval crises of war, plague, and labour conflict
- Tudor centralisation and the Reformation
- the seventeenth-century civil wars, revolution, and constitutional settlement
- the Union, empire, and fiscal-military state
- industrialisation and the Reform Acts
- Victorian expansion, widening franchise, and the age of total war
- the post-1945 welfare settlement, deindustrialisation, devolution, and Brexit
