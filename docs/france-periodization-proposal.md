# France Periodization Proposal

## Purpose

Propose the first honest default lens for dataset 3, `France, 1789-2025`.

This is a higher-reasoning step.
France should not be periodized by equal-duration blocks if doing so blurs the regime breaks and legitimacy crises that make the dataset worth adding.

## Recommendation

Use a `9-era civic lens` for the first France seed pass.

Why:
- France is structurally defined by repeated regime change
- public understanding of France is strongly organized around revolution, republic, empire, occupation, and constitutional refounding
- a longer equal-duration lens would flatten the very transitions the product needs to make legible

## Proposed eras

1. `1789-1799` — Revolution and radical refounding
2. `1799-1815` — Consulate, empire, and continental war
3. `1815-1848` — Restoration, monarchy, and recurrent rupture
4. `1848-1871` — Republic, empire, and defeat
5. `1871-1914` — Third Republic, secular nation-building, and industrial society
6. `1914-1940` — War, instability, and republican fracture
7. `1940-1958` — Occupation, liberation, Fourth Republic, and empire crisis
8. `1958-1981` — Fifth Republic, decolonization, and technocratic order
9. `1981-2025` — European integration, identity strain, and fragmented legitimacy

## Why this lens works

It follows the strongest civic and institutional breaks in the modern French story:
- the Revolution
- Napoleonic consolidation
- restoration and monarchical instability
- the 1848 rupture and Second Empire
- the long Third Republic
- the combined shock of world war and collapse
- Vichy, liberation, and the Fourth Republic
- the 1958 constitutional reset
- the late-modern field of Europe, identity conflict, and legitimacy strain

## Why not force an 8-era or equal-duration model

France is not useful as dataset 3 if it is squeezed into the same shape as Britain or USA.

That would:
- understate regime turnover
- weaken the legitimacy story
- flatten the relationship between republic, empire, and constitutional reset

The right lesson from dataset 3 should be:
- the tool must support different honest lenses
- comparability comes from structure and standards, not identical period counts

## Tradeoffs

Benefits:
- historically legible to non-specialists
- strong for question-led entry
- strong for legitimacy and regime comparison
- well suited to future Britain/USA comparison without pretending they share the same historical rhythm

Costs:
- non-equal period lengths
- slightly higher interpretive burden in the middle nineteenth century
- stronger dependence on a clear seeding plan before runtime data exists

## Tool implication

France strengthens an important product rule:

- dataset switching can already handle different period counts
- future chart logic should also remain honest about non-equal historical durations

The current tool can still display France as lens units if needed, but the planning layer should not pretend those units are equal-duration historical blocks.

## Source basis

## Status

This is the recommended default lens for France planning v1.
It should be treated as `source-backed planning`, not yet as locked runtime data.
