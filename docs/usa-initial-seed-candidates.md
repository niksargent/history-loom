# USA Initial Seed Candidates

## Purpose

Define the first source-backed anchor-event shortlist for dataset 2.

These are planning candidates for the first USA seed layer.
They are not yet live runtime data.

## Seed posture

Each period should start with a small number of anchor events that are:
- historically central
- legible to a general audience
- useful for pressure grounding
- defensible from stable sources

## Proposed first anchor set

### 1. 1776-1789 — Revolution and constitutional founding

- `Declaration of Independence (1776)`
  Why: foundational legitimacy claim, rights language, separation from Britain.
- `Constitution and Bill of Rights (1787-1791)`
  Why: creates federal order while formalizing rights and the architecture of state power.

### 2. 1789-1850 — republic-building, territorial expansion, and sectional strain

- `Louisiana Purchase (1803)`
  Why: accelerates continental scale, executive ambition, and expansion logic.
- `Mexican-American War / Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo (1846-1848)`
  Why: materially extends the republic while intensifying the slavery question and sectional conflict.

### 3. 1850-1877 — slavery crisis, civil war, and reconstruction

- `Emancipation Proclamation (1863)`
  Why: transforms the war into a struggle over freedom as well as union.
- `14th Amendment (1868)`
  Why: recasts citizenship, civil rights, and the meaning of the federal state.

### 4. 1877-1929 — industrial concentration, segregation, reform, and imperial turn

- `Sherman Anti-Trust Act (1890)`
  Why: recognizes monopoly power as a national political problem.
- `Plessy v. Ferguson (1896)`
  Why: legally entrenches segregation and narrows the practical reach of postwar equality.
- `19th Amendment (1920)`
  Why: expands formal political membership and marks a major democratic opening.

### 5. 1929-1945 — depression, federal expansion, and world war

- `Social Security Act (1935)`
  Why: enlarges the federal welfare and administrative state.
- `Roosevelt's "Day of Infamy" address / U.S. entry into World War II (1941)`
  Why: signals wartime mobilization and global state projection.

### 6. 1945-1968 — postwar order, cold war, and civil rights transformation

- `Civil Rights Act (1964)`
  Why: national state power turns directly against legalized racial exclusion.
- `Voting Rights Act (1965)`
  Why: federal protection of political inclusion becomes materially enforceable.

### 7. 1968-2001 — realignment, distrust, neoliberal turn, and media fracture

- `United States presidential election of 1968`
  Why: crystallizes backlash, fragmentation, and long-run political realignment.
- `Watergate scandal (1972-1974)`
  Why: deepens distrust of institutions and executive legitimacy.

Optional next anchor for this period:
- `financial crisis of 2007-08` is historically crucial, but it belongs more naturally to the next period if the lens turns on 2001.

### 8. 2001-2025 — security state, platform era, and polarization

- `September 11 attacks (2001)`
  Why: reorders security, war, surveillance, and executive power.
- `financial crisis of 2007-08`
  Why: exposes fragility in the economic order and intensifies inequality and distrust.
- `United States presidential election of 2016`
  Why: marks a major break in party alignment, media environment, and democratic confidence.

## First pressure-grounding expectations

The first USA seed should especially be able to ground:
- `institutionalLegitimacy`
- `inequality`
- `identityConflict`
- `informationAcceleration`
- `technologicalDisruption`
- `warPressure`

## Notes on restraint

- Not every famous event belongs in the first seed layer.
- The goal is not completeness.
- The goal is a first anchor set strong enough to support the product honestly.

## Status

This shortlist is the recommended starting point for source-backed USA seed authoring.
It should be reviewed before any runtime USA period or event JSON is created.
