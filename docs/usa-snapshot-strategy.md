# USA Snapshot Strategy

## Purpose

Define how the first USA dataset should gain human texture without overloading the initial seed.

## Recommendation

Start with `one primary snapshot per period`, matching the Britain baseline.

Each snapshot should answer:
- what everyday life feels like
- what the system asks of ordinary people
- what seems stable
- what seems unfair, brittle, or newly possible

## Tone

The snapshot layer should be:
- plain-language
- vivid but controlled
- accessible to younger and non-specialist audiences
- compatible with a later `vox pop` or lived-voice layer

## Suggested perspective mix

Across the first 8 USA eras, vary the implied standpoint:
- farmer or artisan in the early republic
- enslaved or free Black perspective during the slavery crisis
- industrial worker, immigrant, or reformer in the Gilded / Progressive era
- unemployed worker, union household, or draftee in the depression and war era
- suburban household, civil-rights activist, soldier, or media consumer in the postwar period
- deindustrializing worker, Sun Belt voter, office worker, or cable-news citizen in the late-20th-century period
- post-9/11 commuter, indebted household, gig worker, or platform-era voter in the contemporary period

## Guardrail

Snapshots should not pretend to represent every American.
They are lenses into lived structure, not total summaries.

## Immediate output for USA seed v1

Create:
- 8 period snapshots
- 1 plain-language synopsis per snapshot
- optional internal notes on whose viewpoint is implied

## Status

This strategy is approved for USA seed planning.
It should feed the first snapshot authoring pass after periodization and anchor events are accepted.
